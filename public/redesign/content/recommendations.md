# Random Recommendations

> This page is intentionally off-topic from Kindle modding. It is just a fun shoutout list of projects and apps I personally love and recommend.

## Projects & Apps I Love

Audiobookshelf (Open Source)

Audiobookshelf is one of my favorite self-hosted apps. It is a great way to manage and stream your audiobook collection with a clean, easy interface.

I also love how flexible it is. Depending on your setup, it can fit nicely into some Kindle audio workflows too.

<https://www.audiobookshelf.org/>

Still (iPhone Audiobookshelf Client) (Not Open Source)

Still is my favorite iPhone app for Audiobookshelf. The developer is very responsive, and the UI/UX is the best iOS audiobook experience I have used so far.

Its free tier already includes the features most people need, including downloads, while some paid alternatives lock basic features behind subscriptions.

<https://still.宙.space>

Calibre-Web (Open Source)

Calibre-Web is another project I strongly recommend, especially if you already use Calibre and self-host your library.

It feels like having your own Kindle-store-style interface for your personal collection. It also supports OPDS, so lots of reading apps can connect to it directly.

<https://github.com/janeczku/calibre-web>

Claude Usage CLI (Open Source)

My favorite Claude usage tracker. Look, I know what you're thinking—this is basically the "Hello World" for every vibe coder on Reddit right now. But trust me bro, this one is actually good because I helped with it!

<https://github.com/NemesisHubris/Claude-Usage-CLI>

Claude Desktop for Linux (Open Source Wrapper)

It is literally the Claude app, but just for Linux! It provides a native-feeling experience with support for MCP and other desktop features on Debian, Ubuntu, and Fedora.

<https://github.com/aaddrick/claude-desktop-debian>
